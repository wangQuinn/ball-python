Different Morphs Of Ball Pythons

"Axanthic" Bradforc Cole, INC. (n.d.). Axanthic-Super-Pastel [Photograph]. World Of Ball Pythons. https://www.worldofballpythons.com/morphs/axanthic-super-pastel/
"Albino" Velvet Reptiles. (n.d.). Albino [Photograph]. World Of Ball Pythons. https://worldofballpythons.com/morphs/albino/
"Pie Bald" Dean Reptiles. (n.d.). Piebald [Photograph]. World Of Ball Pythons. https://www.worldofballpythons.com/morphs/piebald/

The pythons you see up above are probably the three most common ball python morphs that you will encounter! They are:

  • Axanthic - pythons that lack colour pigmentation, resulting in a snake that is various shades of grey
  • Albino - when pythons lack pigmentation, resulting in a light colouring with red eyes
  • Piebald - a pattern where there are some white patches with patches of the original colour.
"Banana" KGB Reptiles. (n.d.). Super Banana [Photograph]. World Of Ball Pythons. https://www.worldofballpythons.com/morphs/super-banana/
"Lemon Blast" Castropica. (n.d.). Lemon blast [Photograph]. World of Ball Pythons. https://www.worldofballpythons.com/morphs/lemon-blast/
"Clown" Surreal Serpents. (n.d.). Clown [Photograph]. World Of Ball Pythons. https://www.worldofballpythons.com/morphs/clown/

These pythons can be grouped as they look pretty similar!

  • Banana - pythons that have a tan base and yellow blotches, with black dots
  • Lemon Blast- bright yellow base with a pinstripe down it‘s back.
  • Clown- a typically brown pattern which can show shades of copper.
"Ivory" Adey & 5Foot16Exotics.com. (n.d.). Ivory [Photograph]. World Of Ball Pythons. https://www.worldofballpythons.com/morphs/ivory/
"Blue-Eyed Leucistic" LimitedEditionReptiles. (n.d.). Blue Eyed Lucy Super Stripe [Photograph]. World Of Ball Pythons. https://www.worldofballpythons.com/morphs/blue-eyed-lucy-super-stripe/
"Scaleless" Aaron Jones. (n.d.). Scaleless [Photograph]. World Of Ball Pythons. https://www.worldofballpythons.com/morphs/scaleless/

This is the last group of snakes, they are all very unique in their own ways!

  • Ivory - a striking off white colouring with hints of beige and peach!
  • Blue-Eyed Leucistic - this morph occurs when there is no pigmentation, called leucism!
  • Scaleless- this morph has no scales, making it very unique!
